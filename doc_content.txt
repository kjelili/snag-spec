App Concept: Snag-to-Spec
One-Line Pitch
Snag-to-Spec is an AI-powered contract intelligence platform that transforms construction defects (“snags”) into contract-compliant Architect’s Instructions, automatically linked to the correct clauses in the JCT Building Contract, saving architects and contract administrators hours per project and reducing dispute risk.
🎯 Core Problem It Solves (Expanded)
Current reality:
Snags are logged in spreadsheets, PDFs, or site apps.
Contract clauses are checked manually (or not at all).
Architect’s Instructions (AIs) are drafted from templates under time pressure.
Mis-referenced clauses → invalid instructions, disputes, or payment issues.
Snag-to-Spec closes the gap between site reality and contractual authority.
🧠 How the App Works (End-to-End)
1. Snag Capture (Site or Desktop)
Architect or CA logs a snag:
Text (“Poorly installed fire stopping at riser – Level 3”)
Photos / video
Location (drawing ref / room / grid)
Severity (cosmetic / safety / compliance)
Supports:
Mobile (tablet/phone)
Voice-to-text on site
Bulk snag import (Excel / Procore / Fieldwire)
2. Contract Intelligence Engine
The AI analyses:
Snag description
Project metadata (contract type, employer’s requirements)
Selected JCT form (e.g. SBC, DB, ICD)
It then:
Identifies relevant clauses (e.g. workmanship, materials, statutory compliance)
Determines instruction authority (Architect vs Contract Administrator)
Confirms whether it is:
A defect obligation
A compliance issue
A change (⚠ potential variation)
3. Clause-Linked Snag Validation
Each snag is automatically tagged with:
Clause number(s)
Clause summary (plain English)
Risk flag:
🟢 Clear defect
🟡 Ambiguous (needs human confirmation)
🔴 Potential variation / cost implication
This protects the architect from accidentally issuing an invalid instruction.
4. Auto-Generated Architect’s Instruction (AI-Drafted)
With one click, the app generates:
Fully formatted Architect’s Instruction
Correct JCT clause references
Clear instruction wording
Optional compliance deadline
Optional inspection re-signoff requirement
Output formats:
PDF (signed / unsigned)
Word
Email-ready
Common Data Environment (CDE) upload
📄 Example Output (Before vs After)
Before (Manual):
“Please rectify defective fire stopping at riser.”
After (Snag-to-Spec):
“Pursuant to Clause 2.1 of the JCT Standard Building Contract, you are instructed to remedy defective fire stopping at Level 3 riser, which does not comply with the Contract Documents or statutory fire safety requirements. Works to be completed within 7 days.”

3) Mapping JCT clauses to defect types (starter ruleset)
Important reality check (so your app stays accurate)
Clause numbering and powers vary by JCT form and edition. Your mapping engine must start by selecting the form + edition and then pull candidate clauses from that edition’s library. JCT’s own 2016 info booklet notes the contractor’s general obligations (e.g., “carry out and complete … in compliance with the Contract Documents” in clause 2.1 across JCT 2016 context). (corporate.jctltd.co.uk)
For SBC/Q 2016 specifically, the contents pages show where “Defects” processes sit (e.g., 2.38 Schedules of defects and instructions and 2.39 Certificate of Making Good). (jctltd.co.uk)
SBC/XQ 2016 contents pages show quality/defects handling clauses around 3.18–3.20 (work not in accordance / workmanship / executed work). (jctltd.co.uk)
JCT commentary on rectification periods reinforces that “rectification period” is not a limit on latent defects liability; it affects the process, not whether defects can exist. (corporate.jctltd.co.uk)
A. Defect-type → clause-tag mapping (form-agnostic)
Build your first pass as defect types → clause tags, not hard-coded numbers:
Non-conforming work/materials → non_conformance, workmanship, materials, removal_replacement
Defects during rectification period → defects, rectification_period, schedule_of_defects, making_good
Compliance/safety (fire, accessibility, statutory) → statutory_compliance, quality, inspection_testing
Incomplete works → completion, practical_completion_support
Damage (post-install) → protection_of_works, make_good_damage
Ambiguity/design discrepancy → contract_documents_priority, instructions, variation_risk
Then your retrieval step fetches the top clauses in the project’s clause library matching those tags.
B. SBC/Q 2016-oriented starter mapping (numbers shown where evidenced by sources)
Use this as an initial “rules” layer for SBC/Q 2016 / SBC/XQ 2016 projects; for DB/MP/IC forms you’ll map to the equivalent clauses via tags.
General non-compliance with Contract Documents
Candidate basis: general obligations (2.1) + “work not in accordance / workmanship / executed work” cluster around 3.18–3.20 in SBC/XQ 2016 contents. (corporate.jctltd.co.uk)
Defect types:
Wrong materials installed
Poor workmanship finish
Incorrect install vs spec/drawings
Missing labels, incomplete builders work to services
Defects process (during/after Practical Completion)
Candidate basis: 2.38 Schedules of defects and instructions + 2.39 Certificate of Making Good shown in SBC/Q 2016 contents. (jctltd.co.uk)
Defect types:
Defects found within rectification period
“Return visit” snag lists
Items needing formal close-out and certification
Rectification period logic (risk / messaging, not a “get out of jail free”)
Candidate basis: JCT commentary on rectification periods and liability concept. (corporate.jctltd.co.uk)
Defect types:
Latent defect alleged after rectification period
